About:


Varkala is a captivating coastal town located in the southwestern state of Kerala, India. Known for its stunning beaches, majestic cliffs, and serene backwaters, Varkala offers a unique and memorable travel experience. The town is situated along the Arabian Sea and is approximately 50 kilometers northwest of the capital city, Thiruvananthapuram. Varkala is renowned for its pristine Papanasam Beach, where pilgrims and tourists alike take a dip in the waters to cleanse their sins, according to local beliefs. The beach is backed by striking red cliffs that provide breathtaking views of the ocean. Nearby, Varkala Cliff is a popular spot for relaxation, shopping, and sampling delicious local cuisine in its numerous cafes and restaurants.

Besides its natural beauty, Varkala is also steeped in history and spirituality. Janardanaswamy Temple, an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, stands as a significant religious site for devotees. The tranquil backwaters of Varkala offer opportunities for peaceful boat rides and witnessing the local fishing culture. Additionally, adventurous souls can indulge in water sports such as parasailing and surfing. Overall, Varkala's relaxed atmosphere, combined with its natural wonders and spiritual essence, makes it a haven for travelers seeking a blend of beachside tranquility and cultural exploration. Whether one seeks relaxation, adventure, or spiritual rejuvenation, Varkala is a destination that leaves an indelible impression on the hearts of those who visit.
